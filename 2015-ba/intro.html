Grading

  • Assignments (20%): You will be given four graded assignments during the semester.
  • Mid-terms (20%): A mid-term quiz.
  • Finals (60%): A final exam and presentation.

Term Project

You will be conducting a term project throughout the semester. There will be four designated assigments according to the project, and one graded presentation at the end of the semester.

Advice on your projects

  • The best topics are the topics you are actually interested in
    • You should be able to "dogfood" your own analysis
  • Don't be afraid to shift the project's direction
    • However, shifting too much will give you less time for real work -- balance!
  • Feel free to use project results in your graduation project or paper
    • Grab two rabbits at once!
    • These projects have potential to become something in your portfolio
    • May be a plus when you get a job, or apply for grad school

Presenting project progress

  • Update your progress (max 10 minutes)
    1. Prepare printouts (for the whole class) or slides or whatever format that best conveys your work
      • Please share your materials at the forum before class
    2. Be brief yet clear
      • Note: Clarity wins brevity
    3. Take notes
      • Keep precise research progress and feedback notes

A good presentation contains the following: 1. What questions you had 1. What approach you chose to alleviate such questions 1. What results you achieved 1. What questions you further got and what you plan to do next 1. Tricks and tips you want to share with the class

Using the forum

How to ask good questions

  • Explicitly list versions of software being used (including packages and OSs)
  • Provide as much details as you can
  • However, remember "brevity and clarity"

What you'll need for this course

  • An OS of your choice (ex: Windows 7, Mac OS X, Ubuntu 14.04)
  • An editor or IDE of your choice

Other notices

  • You are welcome to bring electronic devices to class

  • Never hesitate in asking questions

  • Extra credit will be given to those who rank in Kaggle, or submit their work to a conference or journal

Homework 0: Self introduction

Submit a CV and self-introduction paper in English or Korean (Due: 2015-03-12 23:59)

  1. CV
    • Please include your name, student ID, phone number, email address
    • No constraint on formats, but please try to avoid proprietary formats such as hwp, docx (cf. CVs and file extensions)
      • PDF or HTML
    • No page limits
    • Extra credit for those who include their LinkedIn URL
  2. Self-introduction paper
    • Please include the following:
      1. Your characteristics
      2. Why you're taking this course and what you expect from it
      3. What you wish to do in the future
    • No page limits (Though I don't think I will be reading after page three)

You are seniors! This is a great chance to practice writing and getting feedback on your CV (which you will definitely be sending to companies and/or schools in the near future)

  • Tips on writing a good CV
    • http://tex.stackexchange.com/questions/80/latex-template-for-resume-curriculum-vitae
  • Tips on writing a good self-introduction
    • Use positive words.
    • Divide an conquer!
      • Step 1. Focus only on the contents! (Using a basic text editor or plain paper might be a good idea)
      • Step 2. Do the formatting. Formatting matters. (ex: fonts, layouts, tenses, etc.)
