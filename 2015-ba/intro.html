Assignments (20%)

  • Four graded assignments related to your project during the semester

Mid-term exam (20%)

  • In-class exam covering the first half of the semester
  • Mid-terms will evaluate how you're following up with the course

Finals (60%)

  • Finals consist of an in-class exam covering the second half of the semester, and a project presentation
  • Each regard 30% of the whole score

Term Project

  • You will be conducting a term project throughout the semester
  • There will be four designated assigments according to the project, and one graded presentation at the end of the semester
  • Term projects will be done as individual work
  • Extra credit will be given to those who submit and/or rank in public tournaments (ex: Kaggle)

Advice on your projects

  1. The best topics are the topics you are actually interested in
    • You should be able to "dogfood" your own analysis
  2. Don't be afraid to shift the project's direction
    • However, shifting too much will give you less time for real work -- balance!
  3. Feel free to use project results in your graduation project or paper
    • Grab two rabbits at once!
    • These projects have potential to become something in your portfolio
    • May be a plus when you get a job, or apply for grad school

Presenting project progress

  • Update your progress (Max. 10 minutes)
    1. Prepare printouts (for the whole class) or slides or whatever format that best conveys your work
      • Please share your materials at the forum before class
    2. Be brief yet clear
      • Note: Clarity wins brevity
    3. Take notes
      • Keep precise research progress and feedback notes
  • A good presentation contains the following:
    1. What questions you had
    2. What approach you chose to alleviate such questions
    3. What results you achieved
    4. What questions you further got and what you plan to do next
    5. (Optional) Tricks and tips you want to share with the class

Asking questions

Never hesitate in asking questions

  • Private questions: 2015-ba@dm.snu.ac.kr
    • Personal questions and/or requests
    • Assignment submissions that regard privacy
  • Public questions: 2015-ba@googlegroups.com

      • Provide as much details as you can
      • However, be "brief" and "clear"

Homework 0: Self introduction

Submit a CV and self-introduction paper in English or Korean (Due: 2015-03-12 23:59 to 2015-ba@dm.snu.ac.kr)

This homework is not graded. However, you are seniors! Use this opportunity as a chance to practice writing and getting feedback on your CV (which you will definitely be sending to companies and/or schools in the near future)

  1. CV
    • You are recommended to include the following:
      1. Your name, phone number, email address
      2. Education history
        • What high school you attended
        • What courses you loved most, what you acheived
      3. Working experience: Internships, exchanges, etc.
      4. Others
        • Extracurricular activities
        • Honors, awards, language skills etc.
    • No constraint on formats, but please try to avoid proprietary formats such as hwp, docx (cf. CVs and file extensions)
      • PDF or HTML would be great
      • LinkedIn is another option
    • No page limits
  2. Self-introduction paper
    • Please include the following:
      1. Your characteristics
      2. Why you're taking this course and what you expect from it
      3. What you wish to do in the future
    • No page limits (Though I don't think I will be reading after page three)
