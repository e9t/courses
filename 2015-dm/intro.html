Assignments (30%)

  • Six graded take-home assignments for you to submit online
  • Instead of taking a mid-term exam, we will have assignments for reviewing purposes
  • Frankly, this is not for assessment but for you to review and/or preview class materials (성악설 기반)
    • Will be open early, and can be submitted at any time
    • Therefore no excuses for late submissions
  • Open to everyone at all times
  • Note the submission date
    • The quiz will still be open even after the due date but you won't get any credit for solving it

Final exam (30%)

  • In-class exam covering the whole semester
  • Consists of an easy 80%, a relatively hard 20%
    • If you have fully understood the contents of the assignments, the easy 80% wouldn't be a problem

Term Project (40%)

Logistics

  • Proposal (10/40)
    • Individual work
    • Submit your ideas within 1 page
    • Approx. 10 ideas will selected to be carried out as group work
  • Progress presentation (15/40)
    • Group work
    • You will present your project progress in front of the class (Max. 10 min)
    • You will also be grading your peers' work (Peer assessment)
      • You will be given three votes
      • You can give one vote to three peers, or give all votes to one peer
  • Final report (15/40)
    • Group work

Presentation guidelines

  1. Prepare printouts (for the whole class) or slides or whatever format that best conveys your work
    • Please share your materials to the forum before class
  2. Be brief yet clear
    • Note: Clarity wins brevity
  3. Record the feedback
    • One can prepare for the presentation
    • Another person can do the presentation
    • And another can take feedback notes
  4. A good presentation contains the following:
    1. What questions you had
    2. What approach you chose to alleviate such questions
    3. What results you achieved
    4. What questions you further got and what you plan to do next
    5. Tricks and tips you want to share with the class
  5. Extra credit will be given to those who submit and/or rank in an open tournament (ex: Kaggle)

More advice on your projects

  1. The best topics are the topics you are actually interested in
    • You should be able to "dogfood" your own analysis
  2. Don't be afraid to shift the project's direction
    • However, shifting too much will give you less time for real work -- balance!
  3. Feel free to use project results in your graduation project or paper
    • Grab two rabbits at once!
    • These projects have potential to become something in your portfolio
    • May be a plus when you get a job, or apply for grad school

Homework 0: Self introduction

Submit a CV and self-introduction paper in English or Korean (Due: 2015-03-11 23:59 to 2015-dm@dm.snu.ac.kr)

This homework is not graded. Use this opportunity as a chance to practice writing and getting feedback on your CV (which you will definitely be sending to companies and/or schools in the near future).

  1. CV
    • You are recommended include the following:
      1. Your name, phone number, email address
      2. Education history
        • What high school you attended
        • What courses you loved most, what you acheived
      3. Working experience: Internships, exchanges, etc.
      4. Others
        • Extracurricular activities
        • Honors, awards, language skills etc.
    • No constraint on formats, but please try to avoid proprietary formats such as hwp, docx (cf. CVs and file extensions)
      • PDF or HTML would be great
      • LinkedIn is another option
    • No page limits
  2. Self-introduction paper
    • Please include the following:
      1. Your characteristics
      2. Why you're taking this course and what you expect from it
      3. What you wish to do in the future
    • No page limits (Though I don't think I will be reading after page three)
